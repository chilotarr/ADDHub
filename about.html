About Us.

ADHD is a misdiagnosed condition that affects approximately 4% of adults.

Despite this, boys are more likely than girls to be diagnosed. This is because girls are more inclined to display signs of inattentiveness only and are less likely to exhibit disruptive behaviour, which highlights ADHD symptoms. As a result, girls with ADHD may not always be diagnosed.

AG8 is a group of four international students with the vision to raise awareness of ADHD, assist with issues associated to the disorder, such as time management, focus, and organisation, and improve members' quality of life.

Our intention is to help girls track symptoms and determine their level of ADHD, get personalised feedback, connect with support groups, get self-help tools/resources, develop and most importantly, live productive lives.
